The declaration of human rights is a document that gives every single human being rights and equality, protecting the rights of people everywhere.

The declaration of human rights was adopted on the 10th of December in 1948. It became a thing because of the effects of World War 2.

Work on the Declaration of Human Rights started in 1946. People from all around the world contributed to the writing of the document.

The Declaration gives us 30 rights that no one is allowed to take away from us.

The 30 rights are:

  1. All human beings are free and equal

    Humans are free and are all equal


  2. No discrimination

    Everyone is entitled to all the rights and freedom, regardless of race, sex, or any other individual factor that differentiates one from others


  3. Right to life

    Everyone has the right to life, liberty and security of person.


  4. No slavery

    No one is to be forced to be a slave against their own will.


  5. No torture and inhuman treatment

    No one shall be subjected to torture or to cruel treatment or punishment.


  6. Same right to use law

    Everyone has the right to recognition everywhere as a person before the law.


  7. Equal before the law

    Everyione is entitled to equal protection of law.


  8. Right to be treated fair by court

    Everyone has the right to be treated fairly by court


  9. No unfair detainment

    No one shall be subjected to random arrest, detention or exile.


  10. Right to trial

    The right to a fair hearing.

    1. You have the right to a fair and public hearing by an independent and impartial court.

    2. You have the right to be presumed iunnocent until you're proven guilty according to the law.

    3. You have the right not to be forced to be a witness or to admit guilt.


  11. Innocent until proven guilty

    You have the right to be presumed innocent until you are proven guilty.


  12. Right to privacy

    1. No one shall be subjected to random interference with their privacy, family, home, or correspondence.

    2. There also should be no attacks upon their honour and reputation.

    3. Everyone has the right to the protection of the law against such interference or attacks.


  13. Freedom to movement and residence

    Everyone has the right of freedom of movement and residence within the borders of their own state. Everyone has the right to leave any country, including their own, and return to their country.


  14. Right to asylum

    Everyone has a right to be able to stay in a country's asylum.


  15. Right to nationality

    Everyone has the right to a nationality. No one should have their nationality taken away. Also no one should be denied the right to change their nationality.


  16. Rights to marry and have family

    Men and women of legal age have the right to marry and create a family.


  17. Right to own things

    Everyone has the right to own their things, as well as sharing the ownership of the item with others. No one shall have their property randomly taken away from them.


  18. Freedom of thought and religion

    Everyone has the right to have freedom of thought and religion.


  19. Freedom of opinion and expression

    Everyone has a right to their opinion and to be able to express their opinion.


  20. Right to assemble

    Everyone has the right to freedom of peaceful assembly and association. No one may be forced to belong to an association.


  21. Right to democracy

    1. Everyone has the right to take part in the government of their country, directly or through chosen representatives.

    2. Everyone has the right of equal access to public service in their country.


  22. Right to social security

    Everyone, as a member of society, has the right to social security.

    This includes...

    1. The right to security in the event of unemployment.

    2. The right to security in the event of sickness.

    3. The right to security in the event of disability.

    4. The right to security in the event of widowhood.

    5. The right to security in the event of old age.

    6. The right to security in the event of other lack of livelihood in circumstances beyond their control.


  23. Right to work

    You have the right to be able to work, regardless of race, gender, or any other part of a person that would be able to be used to differentiate them.


  24. Right to rest and holiday

    Everyone has the right to rest and leisure, including reasonable limitation of working hours and periodic holidays with pay.


  25. Right of social service

    Everyone has the right to a standard of living adequate for the health and well-being of himself and of his family, including food, clothing, housing and medical care and necessary social services.


  26. Right to education

    You have a right to be educated.


  27. Right of cultural and art

    1. Everyone has the right to freely participate in the cultural life of the community.

    2. Everyone has the right to enjoy the arts.

    3. Everyone has the right to share scientific advancement and it's benefits.

    4. Everyone has the right to the protection of the moral and material interests resulting from any scientific, literary, or artistic production that they are the author of.


  28. Freedom around the world

    The human rights in the DOHR applies everywhere.


  29. Subject to law

    Every human being is subject to the rights and laws in the DOHR


  30. Human rights can't be taken away

    The DOHR rights can't be taken away from anyone on Earth.
